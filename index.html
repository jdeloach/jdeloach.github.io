Research

I am broadly interested in machine learning and how it can leverage big data to learn new knowledge. Much of my recent work in this field has focused on the applications for mobile app vetting and working with large (~9 TB) datasets with questionable ground truth data. I explore questions like what are the best practices for this domain, how is semi-supervised learning applicable, and how can social media data potentially augment this process?

I am a research assistant in the Machine Learning and Bioinformatics Laboratory at Kansas State University and am advised by Dr. Doina Caragea, and colloborate frequently with Dr. Simon Ou.

In April 2016, I was awarded the Raj and Diana Nathan Undergraduate Research Experience Award to continue my work in machine learning, this time fusing crowd source text sentiments about apps with semantic knowledge from the app binary itself to enhance malware detection techniques.
